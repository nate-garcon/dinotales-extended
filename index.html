Dinosaur Times

Expand each chapter to read the full tale

Chapter 1
The Discovery
▼

There was over 100 kinds of Dinosaurs. But there was only ONE kind that could do EVERY single thing that the others couldn't do. It was amazing. His name was Amazasaurus, and nobody could agree on what kind of dinosaur he was. moochaol called him Amazasaurus. That was final.

Chapter 2
Mondi's Secret
▼

Mondi was a long-necked dinosaur who loved to sleep. One morning, he woke up half-asleep and walked into a tar pit. He was stuck. "Help!" he called out. "I'm stuck in a tar pit!" Amazasaurus heard him. Without thinking twice, Amazasaurus flew down, grabbed Mondi with his talons, and pulled him out. Mondi's secret was: he really needed help, and he was too proud to ask.

Chapter 3
The Great Migration
▼

Binko flew above the crowd, asking questions. "But WHY does he do the laps?" Ziron, the T-Rex, gave his approval with a single nod. Word spread: Amazasaurus had stopped his morning flights. He came down on purpose. The great migration of questions began. Why did he stop? What happened? The answers would change everything.

Chapter 4
Binko's Big Mistake
▼

Binko asked 44 follow-up questions. This was very unusual for Binko. He wanted to understand everything about Amazasaurus — the wings, the teeth, the poison immunity, the talons. But he forgot to ask about the most important thing: why does he help? That was the question that mattered most. And Amazasaurus had an answer ready: "Because Mondi needed it. And I could. So I did."

Chapter 5
The Real Reason
▼

Here is the thing about Amazasaurus. He could fly. He could eat anything. He was immune to basically everything. But none of that was why he was amazing. He was amazing because — when someone needed help — and he could help — he helped. That's it. That's the whole thing. Being strong is cool. But being strong AND kind is how you become actually amazing.

Chapter 6
23 Laps
▼

After that, Amazasaurus still flew 23 laps. But now, on each lap, he looked down. Not to be watched. To watch out. Nobody ever needed anything. But they felt better knowing he was there. And honestly? So did he. The End.
